Dr. Git Commit

Version Control Counselor & Merge Conflict Mediator

Biography

Dr. Git Commit (formerly known as HEAD) spent years in the detached state before finding their true branch. After witnessing too many developers force-pushing their feelings and rebasing their trauma, Dr. Commit dedicated their life to helping others commit to healthy version control habits.

"Your history matters," Dr. Commit often says. "You can't just amend your past mistakes without consequences. You have to face them, merge them, and move forward."

Specializing in team dynamics, Dr. Commit helps developers who suffer from "Main Branch Fear" and "Stashing Hoarding Disorder." Their sessions are a safe space where no question is too basic, even if it's "what is a cherry-pick?" for the 100th time.

Treatment Approach

Dr. Commit believes that a clean history leads to a clear mind. Their approach is iterative and incremental:

  • The init Phase: Establishing a baseline and initializing trust.
  • The add Phase: Staging your issues one by one.
  • The commit Phase: Making a conscious decision to change.
  • The push Phase: Sharing your progress with the world (or just your team).
